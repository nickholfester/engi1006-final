State Weather Stations and Weather Data

Data from NOAA website; https://www.noaa.gov/

Tracking changing weather trends is a necessary component to understanding how human activities impact the world in which we live. From temperature
swings to precipitation change, changes in weather patterns can offer scientists a glance into how Earth's climate system adapts and responds to human activity.

To analyze data, one first needs to collect data. The primary source of weather data in the US comes from hundreds of weather stations spread out across
each state. To meaningfully analyze climate trends, it is of upmost importance to be able to locate weather stations geographically so that data may be meaningfully
collected and cross examined.

This page allows for visualization of the weather stations in sample states.
